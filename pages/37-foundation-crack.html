Foundation cracks can be alarming, but many are normal settling and not cause for panic. Learning to identify the type of crack helps you know when to monitor versus when to call a professional.

Types of Foundation Cracks

Hairline cracks (< 1/8") ‚Üí Usually normal settling‚Äîmonitor
Vertical cracks ‚Üí Often from settling‚Äîmonitor or seal
Horizontal cracks ‚Üí More serious‚Äîcall a professional
Stair-step cracks (in block) ‚Üí May indicate movement‚Äîget inspected
Cracks with bowing walls ‚Üí Structural concern‚Äîcall immediately

What to Do When You Find a Crack

  1. Measure and document ‚Äî Take photos with a ruler for scale.
  2. Mark the ends ‚Äî Use a pencil to mark where the crack ends, with a date.
  3. Check again in 30 days ‚Äî Has it grown past your marks?
  4. Note any water intrusion ‚Äî Wet cracks need sealing regardless.
  5. Get a professional opinion ‚Äî If it's growing or you're concerned.

Warning Signs That Need Immediate Attention

  • Cracks wider than 1/4 inch
  • Cracks that are growing noticeably
  • Doors or windows that suddenly stick
  • Walls separating from ceiling or floor
  • Uneven or sloping floors
