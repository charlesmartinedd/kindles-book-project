**Extracted from Kindle Martin's source documents on December 3, 2025**

## Product Overview

**Title**: Know Your Homebook: A Calm Homeowner's Guide & Maintenance Binder

**Purpose**: A guided, fill-in workbook + binder system that helps homeowners:
- Understand key components of their home
- Know where things are and what they're called
- Track maintenance, repairs, warranties, and providers
- Feel calmer and more confident with emergencies and repairs
- Preserve value and documentation for refinance, HELOC, or sale

**Target User**:
- First-time or non-DIY homeowners
- Busy professionals (healthcare, tech, etc.) with little construction knowledge
- People who want structure + calm, not overwhelm

#### Page 28: WHAT TO DO IF YOU SEE A WATER SPOT ON THE CEILING
- **Header**: WHAT TO DO IF YOU SEE A WATER SPOT ON THE CEILING
- **Subheader**: How to respond calmly and quickly.

**Intro Text**:
> Seeing a water spot on your ceiling can be scary, but it doesn't automatically mean disaster. This page walks you through what it might mean, what to do first, and who to call. Take it step by step—you're not expected to fix it yourself, just to respond calmly and protect your home.

**Section: What This Might Mean**
- A roof leak (missing shingles, damaged flashing, worn materials)
- A plumbing leak from above
- HVAC condensation issue
- Old leak showing through

**Section: Step-by-Step Response**
1. Check if it's active (damp or dry?)
2. Look at what's directly above
3. Contain any active water (bucket, towels)
4. Turn off water if you suspect plumbing
5. Take photos and notes

**Section: Who to Call**
- Under bathroom/kitchen → Plumber
- Under roofline/after storm → Roofer
- Near HVAC → HVAC Technician

**Section: After the Pro Visit**
- What was the cause?
- What was repaired?
- Any follow-up needed?
- Insurance claim needed?
